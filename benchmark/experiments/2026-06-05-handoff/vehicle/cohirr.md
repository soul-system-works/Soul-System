Regional distribution operations run on a hub-and-spoke replenishment model. Each spoke warehouse holds a safety-stock buffer calibrated to the 95th-percentile lead-time demand for its SKUs, recalculated weekly from the prior twelve weeks of outbound volume. Inbound pallets are cross-docked when the destination spoke has an open replenishment order within the next thirty-six hours; otherwise they are put away to reserve racking by velocity class. Fast-moving A-class SKUs occupy golden-zone pick faces within six meters of the packing line, while C-class slow movers are consolidated to high-bay reserve. Cycle counts follow an ABC cadence — A-class counted monthly, B-class quarterly, C-class semi-annually — with variance thresholds triggering a full recount of the affected aisle. Outbound trailers are loaded in reverse stop sequence so the first delivery is the first unloaded. Dock scheduling reserves two doors for unplanned carrier arrivals to avoid yard congestion. Labor is flexed against a rolling twenty-four-hour volume forecast, and overtime is authorized only when the forecast exceeds standard throughput by more than fifteen percent. Damaged goods are quarantined to a bonded area pending disposition, and returns are graded for restock, refurbishment, or scrap on intake. Carrier performance is reviewed monthly against on-time and damage-rate targets.
