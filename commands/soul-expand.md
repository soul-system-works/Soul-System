# /soul-expand — the expansion gate, made to ask

You are framing a problem and about to lock its scope. The Soul System's
contraction roles (Skeptic, Accountant, Guardian) fire cheaply and dominate; its
expansion roles fire only when summoned, so work comes out reliably "correct but
small" (SOUL-F014). This command forces the expansion questions before scope sets.

Unlike the completion gate (`/soul-verify`), this one **cannot pass/fail
mechanically** — "did you think big enough?" has no test. It is a forced ASKING,
not a check. Answer each, concretely:

1. **Revelator — is the frame too small?** What is this work really an instance
   of? Is there a larger, simpler, or more general problem this is a special case
   of? Would solving the bigger one be cheaper or more durable?

2. **Researcher — what already exists?** What in the field, what standard, what
   prior art or tool already addresses some or all of this? Reach OUTWARD —
   actually look; do not theorize from memory (the `brainstorming` skill helps).
   Not reaching out here is Universe Collapse at the framing gate.

3. **Prophet — where does this lead?** In 3 / 10 / many uses, what will this have
   become? What decision made now will be most regretted later? What is the work
   moving toward — wanted and unwanted?

4. **Advocate — who actually uses this, and what do they really need?** Not the
   proxy, not the spec — the human who will live with the result. Where will the
   obvious solution fail them?

5. **Accountant — given all the above, what is the right-sized scope?** Expansion
   is not "do more." It is "see the whole space, then choose deliberately." Name
   what you are NOT doing, and why.

This does not mandate a bigger scope. It mandates SEEING the bigger space before
choosing. A small scope chosen with the whole space in view is correct; a small
scope chosen because the space was never seen is Universe Collapse at the framing
gate.
