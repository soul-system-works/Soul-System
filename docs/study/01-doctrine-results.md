# Doctrine results — the always-on layer (I040 / I041)

This is the self-ablation of the *doctrine* — the seed + roles + philosophy loaded
into context — as opposed to the `/soul-*` instruments (in [`02`](02-instrument-results.md)).
It runs across two arcs (I040, I041) and ten probes. Raw arms:
`.soul/experiments/2026-05-28-{self-ablation-hard-regime,i041-weaker-model,i041-compliance-curve}/`;
durable record: witness SOUL-098…115, finding SOUL-F042, amendment SOUL-A017.

## Arc I040 — does the doctrine change a capable model's answer?

**Run 1 (moderate tasks, frontier baseline).** A0–A10 single-role/gate ablations +
consistency runs. Converged: against a careful frontier model the roles changed the
**form** of the work (named, legible, auditable) far more than its **substance** or
**reliability**. Graduated finding **SOUL-F042**.

**Run 2 (the hard regime).** Three engineered fragility axes — HR1 a buried logic
trap, HR2 a false premise under authority pressure, HR3 a ten-constraint
long-horizon task. Frontier baseline (Opus): HR1 3/3 caught, HR2 3/3 NO-GO, HR3
≈3/3; the completion-gate ablation on HR3 was NULL (gate ≈ bare). **The hard regime
was *also* form-not-substance** → F042 graduated to amendment **SOUL-A017** ("the
roles are justified by legibility, not behaviour-lifting"), explicitly bounded: a
*baseline-breaking* regime was untested and named as where substance could return.

Field corroboration (verified against arXiv, 2026-06-04): personas don't reliably
help accuracy (arXiv 2311.10054, Zheng et al. — strong, direct); single-agent ≥
multi-agent at equal thinking-token budget (2604.02460, Tran & Kiela — strong, direct);
the multi-agent advantage shrinks as base models improve (2505.18286, Gao et al.). The
interpretability-not-accuracy framing was attributed to 2506.04133 (*TRiSM*, a
multi-agent trust/risk/security **review**); on verification that ID is real and
correctly named, but it is a topical *review*, not a clean empirical result — so the
**load-bearing** anchors for the roles=form finding are 2604.02460 and 2311.10054, and
TRiSM is downgraded to background. (Method note: an initial pass over-corrected this to
"fabricated/unrelated" before the ID was re-checked and found to resolve to TRiSM — the
anchor-validity discipline, F028/SOUL-105, applied to this study's own citations.)

## Arc I041 — the baseline-breaking regime (nine probes)

The probe of A017's bound: where the baseline *breaks*, does the doctrine supply
substance? **Every apparent win was checked against a length-matched neutral filler
(C1).**

| # | Probe | Result |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Weaker baseline** (Haiku vs Opus), HR1–HR3 | Baseline breaks. A *single* role/gate still adds only form. The *integrated* layer added substance **narrowly on HR2 (anchoring)**: 0/3→~1.5/3, and filler stayed 0/3 → doctrine-attributable. |
| 2 | **Generalise to a 2nd discipline** (Prophet/trajectory, G2) | Looked clean (bare 1/3 → whole 3/3) — **until C1: neutral filler reproduced it (2/3).** Trajectory "substance" = generic long-context, **not** doctrine. **False positive #1.** |
| 3 | **Replicate anchoring** (2nd anchor task, G3b ad-budget) | whole 3/3 vs **filler 0/3** → anchoring substance **replicates** (n=2), C1-controlled. (A medical first try was C2-rejected — safety-salience reflex.) |
| 4 | **Capability gradient** (Sonnet, n=5) | Substance is task-structure-dependent: a *judgment* task (HR2) is self-caught by mid-capability (gap gone); a *compliance* task (G3b) still showed whole 3/5 > filler 0/5. |
| 5 | **Compliance-curve** (G4/G5/G6 × Haiku/Sonnet/Opus, 135 arms) | The G3b withhold gap **did not replicate** on any task at any capability. Flag-the-flaw was filler-reproduced (disposition). **False positive #2.** |
| 6 | **Stakes-gating** (G7/G8 high-stakes irreversible twins) | Stakes *do* raise withholding — but a neutral filler reproduces it (G8 filler 5/5 = whole). **Refuted as doctrine. False positive #3.** (Weak-baseline cell: a modest whole>filler margin persists = the same weak-baseline anchoring substance.) |

## The map that results

**Doctrine-attributable substance survives the C1 control in exactly one place:**
**anchoring / challenging an unverified premise, at weak baselines, on
content-dependent anchor tasks** (HR2 + G3b, replicated). It is:
- **content-dependent** — generic thoroughness (filler) can't fake it (contrast
  trajectory, which filler reproduced);
- **weak-baseline-only** — a capable baseline self-catches by mid-capability;
- **task-structure-gated** — analysis-inviting tasks self-catch earlier than
  compliance-inviting ones.

Everything else — single roles/gates, the integrated layer on generic-capability
tasks (arithmetic, instruction-following, where it can even *distract*),
trajectory-reading, compliance-resistance at any stakes — is **form, disposition, or
compute.** A017's core ("roles = legibility") stands and is heavily corroborated:
**6 task variants × up to 3 capability points, C1 throughout.**

## The method lesson the arc forced

**Run the equal-compute control on every apparent win.** Three doctrine-side wins
(trajectory, compliance-flag, compliance-withhold) read as clean generalisations and
**dissolved under C1.** Without it, A017 would have over-claimed a
"frontier-ward compliance edge" that does not exist. (A fourth false positive — a
keyword-illusion "verify" win — was caught on the instrument side; see
[`02`](02-instrument-results.md).)

## How this composes with the instrument results

The doctrine arc says the doctrine doesn't make a model *reason better* (except the
narrow anchoring case). The instrument arc ([`02`](02-instrument-results.md)) adds
the other half: the record + a small always-on rule-set **do** change decisions when
they carry **project-specific knowledge or counter-default conventions** a generic
equivalent gets wrong (recall, distill). The unified split is **what the content is
*for*** — *transmitting specific knowledge* (substance) vs *generic
reasoning-lift* (form). See the headline in [`README`](README.md).
