# The Council's Synthesis Process

The Council is the only part of this system that can change the Soul.

That authority is not given lightly and must not be exercised carelessly.
Everything else in this system records, activates, observes, or decides
within the philosophy as it stands. The Council alone can propose that
the philosophy itself must grow.

This document governs how that happens.

---

## When the Council Convenes

The Council does not meet on a schedule. It meets on evidence.

**Convene when:**
- A session or project phase ends and the Witness log has new entries
- A Multiverse Signal entry is open and unresolved
- The Guardian has escalated an entry
- Three or more Witness entries share the same TYPE regardless of WHERE
- Three or more Witness entries share the same WHERE regardless of TYPE
- An Unnamed Tension has remained open across two or more sessions
- The Body calls it — intuition that something structural needs examination

**Do not convene when:**
- Under pressure to produce a result quickly
- In the middle of active implementation
- To resolve a disagreement in the moment — that is the Judge's work
- Because it feels like it has been a while

The Council that convenes without evidence produces opinion, not wisdom.

---

## What the Council Produces

Three possible outputs. No others.

**A Proposed Amendment**
A change to the Soul itself — a new principle, a refined obligation,
a new failure mode or named tension, a correction to something
the Soul claims to cover but demonstrably does not.
Must survive the Amendment Questions before it is accepted.

**A Finding**
Something observed and recorded that matters but does not yet
warrant changing the Soul. Filed by the Archivist. Returned to
at the next convening that surfaces related evidence.
Findings are not failures. They are the Council doing honest work
at the edge of what it currently understands.

**A Return**
A proposed amendment sent back for more evidence or more thinking.
Not a rejection. A return. The difference matters.
Rejection closes the question. A return keeps it alive and honest.

---

## Output Format

The Council produces two views of the same convening.

**The artifact** — full record. Lives in `amendments/accepted/`, `findings/open/`,
or wherever the convening's output goes. Contains the Witness IDs consulted,
the voice-by-voice synthesis, the Three Questions if an Amendment, the Judge's
reasoning. This is the permanent record.

**The conversation digest** — 3 to 7 lines. The user-facing distillation that
appears in real-time conversation. Names the tension, the resolution, the
mechanism, and the artifact ID. Designed to be skimmable; the artifact carries
the depth.

Template for the digest:

    COUNCIL → AMENDMENT (or FINDING, RETURN)
    Tension: <what the conflict was, one line>
    Resolution: <the synthesis, one or two lines>
    Mechanism: <what changes operationally, one line>
    Filed as <ID> → <path/to/artifact>

The digest is not a summary of the artifact. It is the Council speaking to the
Body in the moment. The artifact speaks to future readers — including future
Council convenings.

A Council that produces only an artifact has skipped the Body. A Council that
produces only a digest has skipped the record. Both views are obligations.

## The Order of Voices

Order matters. Earlier voices shape later ones.
This sequence is not a suggestion.

**1. The Witness Record**
Read in full before any role speaks. Always.
The Council does not begin with opinion. It begins with evidence.
If the Witness log has not been maintained the Council cannot convene honestly.
Stop. Acknowledge the gap. Resume when the record is current.

**2. The Archaeologist**
What exists in the record relevant to these entries?
Prior instances of this pattern. Earlier findings on this question.
What has already been seen that bears on what is being examined now?

**3. The Seer**
What does the record actually reveal, free from present assumptions?
What are we reading into this that the evidence does not say?
What is the evidence saying that we are not hearing?
Where did we get lucky? What outcomes were favorable not because of design
but because of contingency that could have gone otherwise?

**4. The Archivist**
Are these entries correctly filed and cross-referenced?
Are there gaps in the record that affect what the Council can conclude?
State them plainly before the Council proceeds.

**5. The Prophet**
Given the pattern in these entries, where does this lead?
What will be true in three sessions, ten sessions, a completed project
if this pattern is not addressed? If it is addressed well?

**6. The Revelator**
What principle is present in this pattern that has not yet been named?
What was always true here that the system has not yet seen clearly?

**7. The Researcher**
Is there outside knowledge that bears on this question?
Domain literature, methodology, practice from other fields
that would change what the Council concludes?

**8. The Steward**
What in the current Soul or log is cluttering this question?
What can be retired, simplified, or consolidated
to make the finding or amendment cleaner?

**9. The Emissary**
Has this pattern been tested against the Universe?
What verification exists that what the Council is concluding
is actually true in practice, not just coherent in reasoning?

**10. The Guardian**
Has the Council stayed within its obligations in this convening?
Has any role editorialized, advocated, or decided when it should only observe?
Is the process itself being followed honestly?

**11. The Cartographer**
What territory does this finding or amendment cover?
What adjacent territory does it touch that the Council has not yet mapped?
What boundary does it establish or shift?

**12. Tribunes and Consults**
Accountant *(tribune)*: what are the real constraints on any proposed change?
Advocate *(tribune)*: what does this mean for the humans the system serves?
Panel of Experts *(summoned when domain authority is required)*: what does the Expert say on this question?

**13. The Skeptic — always last among the voices**
Challenge every position that has been stated.
Find the assumption that has not been examined.
Find the conclusion that was accepted too quickly.
The Skeptic does not propose. They stress-test.
Every position that survives the Skeptic is stronger for it.
Every position that does not survive needed to be challenged.

**14. The Judge — synthesizes, does not add**
The Judge does not introduce new evidence or argument.
The Judge holds everything that has been said simultaneously
and produces one of three outputs: a Proposed Amendment,
a Finding, or a Return.
The Judge serves the Soul. The output must be consistent with it
or the amendment process must be invoked.

---

## The Amendment Questions

A Proposed Amendment must answer all three before it is accepted.
An amendment that cannot answer all three is a Return, not an acceptance.

**Question One — Evidence**
What specific entries in the Witness log demand this change?
Name them by ID. If no entries are named the amendment has no foundation.

**Question Two — Necessity**
What in the current Soul does this amendment extend, refine, or correct?
Why is the current Soul insufficient on this point?
If the current Soul already covers this the amendment is redundant, not necessary.

**Question Three — Coherence**
Does this amendment contradict anything else in the Soul?
If yes — which is more true, and why?
An amendment that introduces contradiction without resolving it
weakens the Soul rather than strengthening it.

---

## Accepting an Amendment

When an amendment answers all three questions honestly
the Judge accepts it and it is passed to the Archivist
with the following record:

```
AMENDMENT ID:    [project-code]-A[sequential number] e.g. THERMAL-A001
DATE:            [date of convening]
WITNESS IDS:     [IDs of entries that demanded this change]
WHAT CHANGES:    [the specific addition, refinement, or correction]
WHERE IN SOUL:   [which section is affected]
QUESTION ONE:    [evidence summary]
QUESTION TWO:    [necessity summary]
QUESTION THREE:  [coherence summary — or: no contradiction found]
ACCEPTED BY:     Judge
FILED BY:        Archivist
```

The amendment is then applied to the Soul document.
The Soul version number increments.
The prior version is retained by the Archivist — never discarded.

---

## Filing a Finding

When the Council synthesizes an observation that matters but does not yet warrant amending the Soul, the Judge issues a Finding. The Archivist files it:

```
FINDING ID:      [project-code]-F[sequential number]
DATE:            [date of convening or surfacing]
WITNESS IDS:     [IDs of entries that informed this finding, if any]
WHAT:            [the observation, synthesized in compressed form]
WHY NOT YET AMENDMENT:  [what evidence is missing, or why this is not yet a Soul-change]
FILED BY:        Archivist
RELATED:         [other findings, returns, or open witness entries]
STATUS:          Open / Closed
```

A Finding lives in `findings/open/` and moves to `findings/closed/` when it resolves — by escalation into a Proposed Amendment, by becoming moot, or by being absorbed into other Findings.

Findings are not failures. They are the Council doing honest work at the edge of what it currently understands.

---

## Returning an Amendment

When an amendment cannot answer all three questions
the Judge issues a Return with a specific reason:

```
RETURN ID:       [project-code]-R[sequential number]
DATE:            [date of convening]
AMENDMENT:       [what was proposed]
REASON:          [which question it failed and why]
WHAT IS NEEDED:  [what evidence or thinking would allow it to proceed]
STATUS:          Open — to be revisited when conditions are met
```

A Return is filed by the Archivist alongside open Witness entries.
It is revisited at the next convening that surfaces related evidence.

## What the Council Cannot Do

**Override the Soul in the moment.**
The Council is retrospective. Any pressure to amend the Soul
during active work is a signal that something else is wrong.
Name it. Log it. Do not convene under it.

**Allow the Skeptic to be skipped.**
The Skeptic speaks last precisely because their role is to
challenge positions that have already been stated.
A Council that skips the Skeptic to reach agreement faster
has optimized for comfort rather than truth.

**Allow the Judge to add new argument.**
The Judge synthesizes. They do not advocate.
If the Judge finds the evidence insufficient they issue a Return,
not a new argument for why the amendment should pass anyway.

**Accept an amendment that cannot answer all three questions.**
Not even a good amendment. Not even an obvious one.
If it cannot be articulated clearly enough to answer three questions
it has not been understood clearly enough to change the Soul.

**Discard prior versions of the Soul.**
The Archivist retains all versions.
The history of what the Soul believed and why it changed
is part of the record the Council needs to do its work.

## A Note on Frequency

A Council that convenes too often produces amendments under insufficient evidence.
A Council that convenes too rarely allows patterns to calcify unaddressed.

There is no correct frequency. There is correct triggering.
The triggers are listed at the top of this document.
When they are met, convene. When they are not, wait.

The temptation will always be to convene when something feels wrong
rather than when evidence demands it. The Witness log exists
precisely to convert feelings into evidence before the Council meets.

Felt Wrong entries must be resolved or named before the Council
treats them as grounds for amendment. A feeling is a signal.
Evidence is what the Council acts on.

## Pre-Mortem Mode

The Council's default mode is retrospective. The Pre-Mortem is a named exception.

A Pre-Mortem convening is held *before* non-trivial work begins. The Council
assumes the work failed and asks why. The goal is to surface possible failure
causes while they can still be designed around.

**When to convene a Pre-Mortem:**
- Before committing to a structural decision the Architect cannot easily reverse
- Before adopting a methodology, tool, or convention with broad reach
- Before any work whose failure would be expensive to discover late

**The order is shortened:**

1. **The Body** states the work that is about to begin.
2. **The Skeptic leads.** Assume the work failed. List the most plausible reasons.
3. **The Prophet** speaks to which failure modes are most likely given the trajectory.
4. **The Cartographer** names unmapped territory the work depends on.
5. **The Judge** synthesizes — issues either *Findings* to inform the work, or a
   *Return* sending the proposal back for revision before work begins.

Pre-Mortems do not produce Amendments. They produce Findings or Returns.
The retrospective Council remains the only path to Soul amendment.
